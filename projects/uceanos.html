Overview

This academic project focused on the structural analysis of a truss bridge design. The objective was to apply principles of statics and mechanics of materials to determine internal forces, stresses, and deformations under various load conditions, ensuring the design met safety and efficiency criteria.

My Role & Contributions

Working individually, my responsibilities included:

  • Developing the initial bridge geometry and member configuration using AutoCAD.
  • Performing hand calculations (method of joints, method of sections) to determine axial forces in truss members.
  • Modeling the bridge structure in SolidWorks and conducting Finite Element Analysis (FEA) to verify hand calculations and identify critical stress points.
  • Using MATLAB to script calculations for varying load scenarios and to plot stress/strain diagrams.
  • Preparing a comprehensive report detailing the design, analysis methodology, results, and recommendations for optimization.

Skills developed

  • Microsoft Excel (for data organization and basic calculations)

Challenges & Solutions

A key challenge was accurately modeling boundary conditions and load applications in the FEA software to match theoretical assumptions. This required careful study of the software's capabilities and multiple iterations of the model. Ensuring convergence of FEA results and interpreting them correctly also demanded meticulous attention to detail.

Outcomes & Learnings

The analysis successfully identified optimal member sizes and connection types for the given load requirements, resulting in a design that was both structurally sound and material-efficient. This project solidified my understanding of structural mechanics, FEA, and the importance of validating simulation results with analytical calculations. It also improved my technical report writing skills.
