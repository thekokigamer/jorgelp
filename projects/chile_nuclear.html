Overview

This project involved the design, construction, and programming of an autonomous rover capable of navigating a predefined course and avoiding obstacles. The primary goal was to develop a functional prototype that could demonstrate basic autonomous navigation principles learned in our robotics course.

My Role & Contributions

As part of a team of three, I was primarily responsible for:

  • Designing the chassis and sensor mounts using SolidWorks, optimized for 3D printing.
  • Integrating ultrasonic sensors for obstacle detection and IR line followers for path navigation.
  • Developing the core navigation logic in Python for the Raspberry Pi controller.
  • Conducting iterative testing and debugging of both hardware and software components.

Challenges & Solutions

One significant challenge was the inconsistent readings from the ultrasonic sensors under varying light conditions. We addressed this by implementing a moving average filter in the sensor data processing logic, which significantly improved reliability. Another challenge was power management for all components; we iterated on battery selection and power distribution to ensure stable operation.

Outcomes & Learnings

The rover successfully navigated the test course 8 out of 10 times, avoiding all major obstacles. This project greatly enhanced my understanding of sensor integration, embedded programming, and the practical challenges of building autonomous systems. I also gained valuable experience in collaborative problem-solving and project management within a team environment.
