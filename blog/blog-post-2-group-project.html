Reflections on a Challenging Group Project

Posted on September 15, 2023 by Your Name

Our recent thermodynamics group project was a rollercoaster, to say the least! The objective was to design and analyze a small-scale power generation system using a thermoelectric generator (TEG). Sounds straightforward, right? Well, the reality was a bit more complex.

The Hurdles We Faced:

  • Data Collection Woes: Accurately measuring temperature differences across the TEG and the resulting power output proved trickier than anticipated. Our initial setup had a lot of heat loss, leading to inconsistent readings.
  • Differing Opinions: As a team, we had some intense debates on the optimal design for the heat sink and the overall system configuration. Reaching a consensus required a lot of discussion and compromise.
  • Time Constraints: Balancing this project with other coursework meant we were often working under pressure, which sometimes led to rushed decisions.

How We Overcame Them:

Despite the challenges, we managed to pull through. Hereâ€™s what worked for us:

  • Improved Communication: We scheduled regular, short meetings to discuss progress, roadblocks, and next steps. Using a shared document for notes and tasks also helped keep everyone on the same page.
  • Iterative Design & Testing: For the heat sink, we decided to 3D print a few different simple designs and test them empirically. This hands-on approach helped us settle on a more effective solution than just relying on theoretical calculations for our small scale.
  • Role Delegation: We clearly defined roles based on individual strengths. One person focused on the experimental setup, another on data analysis and MATLAB scripting, and I took the lead on the CAD modeling and report compilation.
  • Seeking Feedback: We weren't afraid to ask our professor and TA for guidance when we got stuck. Their input was invaluable in pointing us in the right direction, especially with the data interpretation.

Key Takeaways:

This project was a powerful reminder that engineering is often about navigating the unexpected. My main takeaways are:

  1. Teamwork is crucial, but it requires effort: Clear communication, mutual respect, and a willingness to compromise are essential.
  2. Failures are learning opportunities: Our initial failed experiments taught us more than if everything had worked perfectly from the start.
  3. Practical skills complement theory: Being able to quickly prototype and test ideas was a huge advantage.

Ultimately, we submitted a project we were proud of, and the experience taught me a lot about collaboration, resilience, and the practical side of engineering problem-solving. Teamwork really does make the dream work, even when (or especially when) it's tough!
